I have played the bagpipes for over 25 years now, and started when I was a young boy.

That was with my local pipe band where I soon graduated from their novice section to their juvenile section. I have competed with a number of pipe bands with success, winning prizes in nearly every competitive grade going except for grade 1.

I have also been both Pipe Sergeant and Pipe Major of both community based and competition oriented bands, where I was responsible for leading bands on parade and at practices, and helping ensure a musically satisfying performance was achieved. To that end I utilised a number of teaching aids and resources from organisations such as the College of Piping and Royal Scottish Pipe Band Association to develop both learners and more adept playing members abilities.

Away from pipe bands I have spent some time studying pibroch or 'ceol mor' on an individual basis. This is highland clan music that was historically played by soloists to mark special occasions such as winning or losing battles, notable deaths and births, as well as more pastoral fare where tunes commemorate fields, caves and sometimes even cows!

As a solo piper, I have played at many events - weddings, funerals, birthdays and hogmanay are staples where a Scottish flavour is added. Less common is for highland dancers, where particular tunes are requested to match particular dances. I have also branched out into composition - which was a requirement for studying music at high school - and have even released some books of music myself, independently.

Lessons are available to help you achieve your goals, whether they be:

- polishing up band tunes                - learning bagpipes from scratch                - how to compose/write your own tunes                - performing for dancers
- pibroch or 'ceol mor'                     - harmony composition/arrangement         - widening your repertoire of tunes                        - achieving better sound/stability
